# Implementation Plan: Narrative Review & PRISMA-Guided Strategy

This document outlines the framework for the review article:
**Contemporary Breast Cancer Management in the Era of Precision Oncology: Therapeutic Innovations, Barriers to Care, and Future Directions**

**Review Approach:**
Narrative review supported by a structured literature search and PRISMA-guided study selection framework.

## 1. Manuscript Structure

1. Introduction
2. Molecular Landscape of Breast Cancer
3. Advances in Diagnosis and Screening
4. Contemporary Treatment Strategies
5. Emerging Innovations in Precision Oncology
6. Barriers to Optimal Care
7. Future Directions
8. Conclusion

## 2. Search Strings

To ensure comprehensive coverage across the selected databases (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, ScienceDirect), the search strategy will utilize Boolean operators (AND, OR).

**Core Search String:**
`("Breast Cancer" OR "Breast Neoplasm" OR "Breast Carcinoma") AND ("Precision Oncology" OR "Targeted Therapy" OR "Immunotherapy" OR "Antibody-drug conjugates" OR "ctDNA" OR "Artificial Intelligence" OR "Healthcare Disparities" OR "Financial Toxicity" OR "Barriers to Care" OR "HER2-low" OR "PARP inhibitors" OR "ADCs" OR "Trastuzumab Deruxtecan" OR "Sacituzumab Govitecan" OR "Radiomics" OR "Multiomics" OR "Digital Health" OR "Personalized Medicine" OR "CAR-T")`

## 3. Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

**Inclusion Criteria:**
1.  **Publication Date:** January 2023 – Present (2026).
    *   *Exceptions for older landmark references:* GLOBOCAN, NCCN, DESTINY-Breast03, KEYNOTE studies, OlympiA.
2.  **Article Type:** Reviews, clinical trials, meta-analyses, and clinical guidelines.
3.  **Language:** English language only.
4.  **Relevance:** Must directly address contemporary breast cancer management, therapeutic innovations, diagnostic advances, or barriers to care.

**Exclusion Criteria:**
1.  **Article Type:** Conference abstracts/papers, editorials, letters to the editor, short communications, and case reports.
2.  **Language:** Non-English publications.
3.  **Focus:** Pre-clinical (in vitro or animal model) studies without direct clinical translation/relevance.
4.  **Date:** Published prior to 2023 (unless deemed a landmark foundational paper).

## 4. Target Reference Allocation

To maintain a balanced review and allow a buffer for final selection, the strategy is:
*   **Search Target:** 60–70 references
*   **Final Manuscript Target:** 50–55 references

The final 50-55 references will be distributed roughly as follows:
*   Introduction: 4–5 references
*   Molecular Landscape: 4–5 references
*   Diagnosis and Screening: 6–8 references
*   Treatment Strategies: 12–15 references
*   Emerging Innovations in Precision Oncology: 12–15 references
*   Barriers to Care: 4–5 references
*   Future Directions: 4–5 references
*   **Total: ~50-55 references**

## 5. Screening Workflow

1.  **Identification:** Execute search strings across the databases and export results to a reference manager. Remove exact duplicates.
2.  **Phase 1 Screening (Title & Abstract):** Quickly screen titles and abstracts against the inclusion/exclusion criteria.
3.  **Phase 2 Screening (Full-Text):** Retrieve full-text articles for the remaining candidates. Evaluate for depth, quality, and specific relevance to the manuscript's outline.
4.  **Phase 3 Quality Assessment:** Articles will be evaluated for journal quality, citation impact, clinical relevance, methodological rigor, and recency.
5.  **Final Selection:** Generate a shortlist of 50 optimal references, mapping each to a specific manuscript section.
