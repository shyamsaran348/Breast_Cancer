### Table 1: Molecular Subtypes of Breast Cancer
| Subtype | Biomarkers | Clinical Features | Prognosis | Standard Treatment |
| ------- | ---------- | ----------------- | --------- | ------------------ |
| **Luminal A** | ER+, PR+, HER2-, Ki-67 Low | Most common, slow-growing | Best | Endocrine therapy (e.g., Aromatase inhibitors) ± CDK4/6 inhibitors [5, 43] |
| **Luminal B** | ER+, PR±, HER2±, Ki-67 High | Faster growing, larger tumors | Intermediate | Endocrine therapy + Targeted therapy/Chemotherapy [5, 43] |
| **HER2-Enriched** | ER-, PR-, HER2+ | Aggressive growth | Improved with anti-HER2 | Anti-HER2 targeted therapy + Chemotherapy [6] |
| **TNBC** | ER-, PR-, HER2- | Highly aggressive, BRCA1 mutations | Poor; high early relapse | Chemotherapy + Immunotherapy [9] |
| **HER2-Low** | IHC 1+ or 2+/ISH- | Spans HR+ and HR- subtypes | Emerging | Antibody-Drug Conjugates (e.g., T-DXd) [8] |

### Table 2: Landmark Clinical Trials in Precision Breast Oncology
| Trial | Population | Intervention | Outcome | Clinical Impact |
| ----- | ---------- | ------------ | ------- | --------------- |
| **DESTINY-Breast03** | HER2+ Metastatic | Trastuzumab Deruxtecan (T-DXd) | PFS improvement (72% risk reduction) | Established T-DXd as standard-of-care second-line therapy for HER2+ [44] |
| **DESTINY-Breast04** | HER2-Low Advanced | Trastuzumab Deruxtecan (T-DXd) | OS improvement | Redefined HER2-low as a distinct therapeutic category across HR+ and HR- subtypes [8] |
| **KEYNOTE-522** | Early-stage TNBC | Pembrolizumab + Chemotherapy | pCR benefit | Standard of care for early-stage TNBC [45, 46] |
| **KEYNOTE-756** | High-Risk Early ER+/HER2- | Pembrolizumab + Chemotherapy | pCR benefit | Expanding immunotherapy into high-risk luminal breast cancer [20] |
| **TROPiCS-02** | HR+/HER2- Metastatic | Sacituzumab Govitecan | OS improvement | Expanded ADC utility beyond TNBC into pretreated HR+ disease [25] |

### Table 3: Emerging Precision Oncology Technologies
| Technology | Current Status | Clinical Application | Key Limitations |
| ---------- | -------------- | -------------------- | --------------- |
| **Artificial Intelligence** | Early adoption | Screening, diagnosis, decision support [10, 38, 39] | Algorithmic bias, transparency, dataset representation |
| **Radiomics** | Research | Non-invasive risk prediction and tumor phenotyping [12] | Standardization across imaging platforms, massive data needs |
| **Liquid Biopsy (ctDNA)** | Clinical use | MRD monitoring, early resistance detection [14, 15] | Sensitivity limits in early-stage, cost |
| **Multi-omics Integration** | Emerging | Holistic therapy selection, unraveling resistance [41] | High computational cost, integration of disparate data |
| **Digital Twins** | Experimental | *In silico* treatment simulation and virtual models | Need for extensive clinical validation, real-time data input |

### Table 4: Barriers to Care and Mitigation Strategies
| Barrier Domain | Examples | Clinical Consequences | Potential Solutions |
| -------------- | -------- | --------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Socioeconomic** | Financial toxicity, lack of insurance coverage [24, 30] | Treatment non-adherence, delayed prescription fulfillment | Widespread adoption of biosimilars |
| **Healthcare System** | Workforce shortages, limited infrastructure | Delayed care, centralization bottlenecks | Implementation of tele-oncology and digital health platforms |
| **Geographic** | Rural isolation, distance to tertiary cancer centers [29, 28] | Reduced screening participation, delayed diagnosis | Mobile mammography units and robust community screening programs |
| **Diagnostic** | Lack of access to advanced molecular profiling [27] | Suboptimal treatment selection | Decentralized pathology services and point-of-care testing |
